What online route planner to use?

There are many online services that can help you to find or plan a route on map. I recommend to read about some of them in this article.

Also this website can help you to choose a good service.

Google Maps

Google Maps has become a staple for online mapping and its online route planner doesn’t disappoint either.

Google lets you choose from a wide variety of modes of transport – including car, rail, foot, bicycle, and air – using one of the simplest mapping layouts around. You can easily select alternative routes from the map, with a number of customisable route options such as avoiding tolls and motorways. You can even add a stop off too if you like.

There are some traffic warnings on the maps, although they’re not incredibly detailed. You also don’t get predicted fuel costs like other sites.

Michalin's Route Planner

Michelin’s online mapping service lets you generate routes for car, motorbike, bicycle or foot.

This service is great if you’re using a caravan as it’ll allow you to factor the extension into your journey.

ViaMichelin is also print-friendly and provides detailed traffic impacts that may slow your journey down.

Thought you can add no more than 6 stops on your route if you need to make multiple stops you’re in luck..

AA Routeplanner

AA route planner is Ireland route planner. It has been functioning for a very long time, so it is approved by many visitiors. The service has seen many changes in its 100 years which started with early AA Patrols providing motorists with information about the shortest routes for their journey.

If you simply want directions from A to B, the AA’s Route Planner is a great choice. The site is easy to use and made searching for our desired routes a breeze.

Another decisive advantage is that you also can add traffic alerts to let you know of any hold-ups you might encounter, and nearby parking can also be included. The turn-by-turn directions were the best on test, providing images of junction signposts and landmarks, which are clear when printed out, too. There’s no offer of any alternative routes, though, and there are a few annoying adverts on the page.

Waze Online Maps

Waze aimed purely at those traveling on the road and is one of the best ways to avoid traffic, as it’s powered by its userbase. The Waze website allows you to prepare your journey in advance using the data from user’s smartphones to give you the quickest possible route – however, it only appears to work when calculating a route within the same country (so there’s no luck planning a trip across the continent with your car). Sadly it doesn’t format it for printing so it might be worth getting hold of the smartphone apps if you can too.

For this reason Waze’s traffic data is detailed and very up to date. What’s more, you can choose the time you’ll be travelling and it’ll use previous data to predict how the traffic should be. While this is by far Waze’s strength, don’t expect extras like multiple stops, places to get a bite to eat or fuel stop.
