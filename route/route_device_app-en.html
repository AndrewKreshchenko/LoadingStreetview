What route app for device to choose?

Regardless of the operating system on your site (iOS, Android), you will be able to find a large number of offers in digital distribution platform of your mobile (Play Market, App Store etc.) to download the map planner, including Google app. Listed below are applications that are mainly used for both iOS and Android. Although there is one fact: some applications are geared towards the driver, and include the appropriate features, while others are oriented on the regular traveler. Therefore, making a choice, I recommend firstly to find out your needs.

I selected these apps by referring to this website.

OSM (Open street maps) is a collaborative project to create a free editable map of the world. Now OSM app is widespread all over the world both for iOS and Android users. Many software tools and services allow you to use OpenStreetMap data offline, without an Internet connection!

I would recommend to choose MapFactor GPS Navigation app for device with OS Android and Sygic GPS Navigation & Maps for iOS. Though there is one problem: Sygic GPS Navigation & Maps is not usable in non-European countries and Ukraine. Android OSM app can probably be ideal choise for big trips on top.

There are many OSM apps, but every one has its own pros and cons. So it's still better to read review comments and ensure that a selected application will work in a counry you use it.

MapFactor GPS Navigation Maps

MapFactor is the first option in this list to present the ability to use maps provided by OpenStreetMap (OSM) rather than commercially available options, though it does also give you the option of using the map with TomTom data instead of the open-source version and this version is paid.

However, you'll need to pay for certain features, regardless of which map platform you choose while setting up. For example, if you want a heads-up display of your navigation on your windshield, that'll cost you, and if you want the ability to choose between three different pre-planned routes, that's also an added charge — the benefit with MapFactor, however, is that this then allows for offline routing options when you don't have data.

It's certainly nowhere near as well designed or easy to use as some of the alternatives, but if you're an OSM fan, it's a good option. You will have to put up with ads unless you pay to remove them though.

OSMTracker for Android

OSMTracker runs on Android, Windows Mobile. App allows you to track your journeys, mark waypoints with tags, voice record, photos and export them in GPX format for later use with OpenStreetMap tools like JOSM. Tracks can be displayed over OSM maps or with no background if you don't have a data plan. There were fixed bug recently when using internal memory for storage.

Sygic GPS Navigation & Maps

Sygic GPS Navigation & Maps is the world’s most advanced navigation app. Lots of smart navigation features, beautiful 3D offline maps and easy to use interface provide exceptional navigation experience. Get Sygic GPS Navigation and join over 200 million happy drivers. You have to download a map and after you're using it without internet. The application has lots of POI and travel points from famous sources like tripadvisor.

HERE WeGo

Formerly known as HERE Maps, HERE WeGo, is an excellent choice if you need a more robust offline mapping and navigation experience. It's far more like an all-singing, all-dancing navigation app like Google Maps, rather than a single-focus app like Citymapper or Waze. What it ends up offering is a great experience for people that sometimes need in-car navigation and at other times need transit directions.

Also like Google Maps, it'll let you book a taxi (or check out nearby car clubs) directly within the app — it's particularly handy for public transport as it shows the total cost of your chosen route before you depart and gives you notifications of each aspect of your journey as you progress.

Waze Maps

Waze is another Google-owned mapping and navigation app with some serious skills, but it takes an entirely different approach to the company's flagship map app, as it crowdsources all its data and relies on a community of users to keep it up to date with relevant information.

Those with the Waze smartphone app on Android or iOS open as they drive contribute real-time information on the roads, which are give you the chance of finding the least congested journey.

Citymapper

If you're a driver, or out in the countryside, there are better options than Citymapper, but if you're living in a town or city that it operates in, it's an invaluable resource for public transit trips. It'll show you the approximate cost of an Uber to your destination within the app too.

It'll show routes for any type of public transport available, indicating when the next train or bus is due, any delays along the planned route and how much the trip will cost.

With a focus on a simple, easy to use design, Citymapper is highly recommended for when you just need an easy way to get from Point A to Point B.
